Portfolio Narrative

The week was dominated by leasing velocity work, with nearly every weekly ops call converging on the same diagnosis: April tour volume is adequate, but conversion and renewals are under pressure across the portfolio. Management response was to pull the concession lever hard. The Flats and Nightingale are testing up to 8 weeks free; Dallas (Skye Isle/Love and the Walnut portfolio) is rolling out a 6-week prorated concession; Skye Reserve removed the $300 classic-unit premium and cut two-bedroom rents $100-$150; Hanley Place added a 2-week upfront concession to unlock deposit-constrained applicants; Skye Ridge pivoted marketing spend from Apartments.com to PPC/PMAX.

Conway is within striking distance of its 94% target and has a concrete plan to get there this week.

On the capital and structural side, Preserve at Riverwalk's loan modification moved forward with a novel lender-reserve mechanic that preserves relief without requiring fresh equity - Zamir is modeling the recap-savings/shortfall interaction before the credit committee review. Skye Reserve insurance financing with MF1 and the Sky Oaks insurance claim with PE Risk remain open threads. Chimney Hill's post-fire make-ready and potential disposition were both discussed, and a broader refinance/disposition evaluation is underway on Chimney Hill and Skye Isle.

Acquisitions pipeline stayed active: Ashford at Spring Lake (Atlanta industrial corridor, mid-to-high $180M target), Aventerra at Dobson Ranch ($100M / 6% cap, 5/5 bid date), Wesley Village (Charlotte value-add), GF Properties (Dallas), and Suwanee Creek (Alpharetta, core plus).
